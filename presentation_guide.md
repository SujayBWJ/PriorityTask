# Presentation Guide: 4-Person Team Distribution

This guide ensures each team member has a clear, high-impact role during the presentation.

## Speaker 1: The Vision & User Experience (Project Lead)
**Role**: Sets the stage and handles the "Story" of the project.
*   **Intro**: Hook the audience with the problem: "Everyone has 24 hours, but most task managers let you add 30 hours of work. We fixed that."
*   **Demo**: Show the **Landing Page** and the **Login/Registration** flow.
*   **Talking Points**: User privacy, ease of use, and the core goal of "Skill Classes."

## Speaker 2: The Logic Engine (Backend Lead)
**Role**: Explains the "Magic" under the hood.
*   **Deep Dive**: Explain the **Priority-based Auto-Drop Logic**.
*   **Demo**: Add a "High Priority" task that exceeds daily capacity and show the system automatically dropping a "Low Priority" task to the **Impossible** section.
*   **Talking Points**: How we use Python logic to ensure the schedule remains realistic.

## Speaker 3: The Technical Backbone (Systems Architect)
**Role**: Demonstrates the stability and security of the build.
*   **Stack**: Briefly mention the **Flask/SQLite** stack.
*   **Database**: Explain why we used a relational database (SQL) for data integrity.
*   **Security**: Mention password hashing and session management.
*   **Talking Points**: Code modularity, clean architecture, and project reliability.

## Speaker 4: Continuity & Future Growth (Product Manager)
**Role**: Closes the loop and looks forward.
*   **Logic**: Explain the **Backlog Recalculation Engine**.
*   **Demo**: Show the **Recalculate** button catching missed deadlines and moving them to the backlog.
*   **Summary**: Recap the value of the project.
*   **Forward-Looking**: Mention one future feature (e.g., "In V2, we'll add AI-powered habit suggestions").

---
### Presentation Pro-Tips:
1.  **Hand-offs**: Always say "And now, [Name] will explain how we handled the backend logic..."
2.  **Live Demo**: Speaker 2's part is the "Wow" moment—make sure the capacity math is easy for the audience to follow.
3.  **Q&A**: All 4 members should stand together to answer questions based on their focus areas.
